Purbachal International
Convention Centre

Project Overview

The design proposal is an interwoven scheme of built form, non-building spaces, landscape, and culture. The building is conceived as a platform to promote access to everyone — both within and beyond the building. Derived from the concept of rich cultural and architectural precedence of Bangladesh, inspiring and adaptive public spaces are sought to create a new landmark destination and icon.

The project brief includes two identical exhibition halls, one auditorium, two seminar halls, and workshop halls as the main programmatic elements which primarily dictated the design organization. These functions are quite monolithic in nature with varying footprints and rigid shapes. A pragmatic design approach was adopted to house these functions, keeping the simplest form of circulation that ensures the most efficient organization.

This pragmatic approach was inspired by the architectural wisdom of Somapura Mahavihara — the archaeological gem site situated in Naogaon, Bangladesh which dates back to 800 AD. The central functions are placed according to a pure geometric principle inspired by the Vihara and later knitted together by crafting an intricate building skin, which showcases the fusion of rich cultural heritage of Bengal and contemporary design innovation.

The ease of navigation for the users and visitors has been one of the major considerations behind the design scheme — ensuring efficient crowd management from entering the premises to using interior and exterior spaces. The circular form ensures the fluidity of space throughout the building, with the central plaza cum lobby working as an avenue with abundant nature, fresh air, and natural light.
